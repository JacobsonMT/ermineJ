Tutorial: Precision-Recall scoring

Method Overview

Unlike the other methods in ermineJ other than the ROC method, the PRC method uses only the ranks of the gene scores. That is, all it cares about is the ordering of items obtained by your gene scores (e.g., t-test or fold-change), but doesn't use the information about the relative values of the scores.

When to use PRC scoring

Like other non-parametric techniques, using ranks costs some statistical power, but also makes fewer assumptions. Specifically, if you think the ordering of items in your data is more accurate than the actual p-values themselves, the ROC might be appropriate. A nice thing about ROC compared to gene score resampling is that the p-values are more precise, wherease gene score resampling p-value precision is limited by the number of random samples used.
